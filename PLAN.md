# Forge — Personalized Training Plan

**Profile:** Intermediate · 75 kg · ~165 cm · 6 training days + 1 active-recovery day
**Equipment:** Dumbbells + bodyweight · No injuries
**Goals:** Fat loss · lean physique · MMA conditioning · strength · yoga · pranayama

At ~75 kg and ~165 cm your BMI sits around 27, so a *recomposition* approach — losing fat while holding muscle — fits your goals better than either pure bulking or aggressive dieting. Everything below is built around that.

---

## Review of your original plan — and what I changed

Your original split was a solid skeleton. It covered strength, power, MMA, conditioning and recovery, and it already respected rest days. The main gaps I addressed:

**1. Equipment mismatch.** Your plan listed barbell bench press and barbell rows, but you train with dumbbells. Every lift is now programmed for dumbbells, which is actually *better* for you — greater range of motion, balanced left/right development, and joint-friendliness for striking.

**2. Push/pull imbalance.** The original had a lot of pressing (push-ups, bench, shoulder press) but only one rowing movement all week. For a striker that's a recipe for rounded shoulders and shoulder niggles. I added rows on Monday, single-arm rows Saturday, and renegade rows in the conditioning circuit so your back is trained as hard as your chest.

**3. No hip hinge.** There was no deadlift/hinge pattern — a big gap for posterior-chain strength and lower-back health. I added the DB Romanian Deadlift on Tuesday.

**4. Fat loss wasn't explicitly driven.** Fat loss is ~80% kitchen. The training now pairs strength (protects muscle) with HIIT circuits and Zone-2 walking (drives the burn), but the real lever is a **mild calorie deficit with high protein** — covered below.

**5. Added Kalaripayattu, intentionally.** Rather than bolting it on, I wove it where it carries over: *chuvadu* footwork feeds your MMA day, the *vadivu* animal stances build isometric leg/hip strength and balance on mobility days, and the low stances double as deep mobility work.

**6. Sequenced the pranayama by purpose.** Energizing breath (Kapalabhati/Bhastrika) in the morning before training; calming breath (Nadi Shodhana/Bhramari) in the evening and on recovery days. This matches how these techniques affect the nervous system.

**What was already good and kept:** the weekly rhythm, the dedicated MMA and conditioning days, the genuine recovery days (don't skip them — 6 hard days only works if Wed and Sun stay easy), and a strong core emphasis.

## The weekly plan

### Monday — Upper Body (Push & Pull)
- **Warmup:** Arm circles · Scapular wall slides · Push-up to down-dog
- **Workout:** DB Floor/Bench Press 4×10–12 · DB Bent-over Row 4×10–12 · DB Shoulder Press 3×10 · Renegade Row 3×8/side · Push-up burnout 2×max
- **Core:** Plank 3×45s · Hollow hold 3×30s
- **Cooldown:** Chest & lat stretch

### Tuesday — Lower Body (Strength & Power)
- **Warmup:** Leg swings · Bodyweight squats · Kalari low-lunge hold 30s/side
- **Workout:** DB Goblet Squat 4×12 · DB Romanian Deadlift 4×10 · DB Walking Lunge 3×12/leg · Bulgarian Split Squat 3×10/leg · Jump Squat 3×8
- **Core:** Leg raises 3×12 · Russian twists 3×20 · Calf raises 3×20
- **Cooldown:** Hip-flexor & hamstring stretch

### Wednesday — Mobility (Yoga & Kalaripayattu) · *recovery*
- Joint rotations → Surya Namaskar ×5
- **Kalari Vadivu holds:** Gajavadivu (elephant), Ashwavadivu (horse), Simhavadivu (lion) — 45s each
- **Asanas:** Bhujangasana · Cat–Cow · Trikonasana · Pawanmuktasana
- **Pranayama (calming):** Nadi Shodhana 5 min · Bhramari ×9

### Thursday — MMA Skills & Footwork
- **Warmup:** Jump rope 5 min · Kalari chuvadu footwork 5 min
- **Shadowboxing:** 5 × 3-min rounds (fundamentals → movement/angles → freestyle), 1 min rest
- **Conditioning finisher:** Burpees / Mountain Climbers / Jump-rope sprints — 40s on / 20s off × 3
- **Core:** Plank · Side plank · Bicycle crunches

### Friday — Fat-Loss Conditioning Circuit
- 45s work / 15s rest · 5 rounds · 60–90s between rounds
- DB Thruster · Push-ups · DB Renegade Row · Jump Squat · Mountain Climbers
- **Finisher:** 10-min Zone-2 walk or easy jog

### Saturday — Full-Body Functional Strength
- DB Goblet Squat 3×12 · Feet-elevated Push-ups 3×12 · DB Single-arm Row 3×12/side · DB Farmer Carry 3×40m · DB Push Press 3×10
- **Core:** Leg raises 3×12 · Plank shoulder taps 3×30s

### Sunday — Active Recovery & Restoration · *recovery*
- 30–45 min brisk walk (~8000 steps) · 15 min gentle yoga
- Abhyanga (warm-oil self-massage) → warm shower
- **Pranayama:** Anulom Vilom 5 min · Bhramari ×9

## Pranayama protocol

- **Morning (empty stomach, energizing):** Kapalabhati 3 × 30 breaths · Bhastrika 2–3 rounds. Increases alveolar ventilation and blood-oxygen saturation — primes you to train.
- **Evening / recovery (calming):** Nadi Shodhana 5–10 min · Bhramari ×9. Strengthens parasympathetic tone, lowers heart rate and blood pressure, improves sleep.

> **Caution:** Kapalabhati and Bhastrika briefly raise heart rate and blood pressure. Practise on an empty stomach, stop if you feel dizzy, and check with a doctor first if you have high blood pressure, heart or respiratory conditions. This is general guidance, not medical advice.

## Ayurvedic daily rhythm (Dinacharya)

- Wake early; warm water on rising to spark digestion (*agni*).
- Train in the Kapha window (~6–10 am) when you can — physically strongest, best for fat loss.
- Eat to two-thirds full; favour spicy, bitter and astringent foods to balance Kapha.
- Keep dinner lighter and earlier.
- Abhyanga (warm-oil self-massage) on recovery days.
- Protect 7–8 hours of sleep — adaptation happens during rest, not the workout.

## Nutrition targets

| Target | Amount |
|---|---|
| Protein | ~130–150 g/day (≈1.7–2.0 g/kg) to preserve muscle in a deficit |
| Water | 3–4 litres/day |
| Sleep | 7–8 hours/night |
| Calories | Mild deficit (~300–500 kcal under maintenance) for steady fat loss |
| Protein sources | Eggs, chicken, fish, paneer, curd, milk, dal, sprouts, soy |

The single highest-leverage habit for your fat-loss goal is hitting that protein target every day while in a mild deficit — the research consistently shows higher protein (≈1.6–2.4 g/kg) preserves lean mass during weight loss.
